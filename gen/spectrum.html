Electromagnetic Spectrum

This HTML file creates a visual representation of the electromagnetic spectrum. It shows different types of radiation as colored sections, with descriptions of their properties and uses. The visible light part is shown as a rainbow gradient. Other sections use colors to represent their position in the spectrum. You can view this file in a web browser to see the colorful display of the electromagnetic spectrum.

Radio Waves

Wavelength: 1 meter to 100,000 kilometers

Properties: Longest wavelength, lowest energy

Uses: Broadcasting, communications, navigation

Microwaves

Wavelength: 1 millimeter to 1 meter

Properties: Can penetrate clouds, fog, and some materials

Uses: Communications, radar, cooking

Infrared (IR)

Wavelength: 700 nanometers to 1 millimeter

Properties: Thermal radiation

Uses: Thermal imaging, remote controls, heating

Visible Light

Wavelength: 380 to 700 nanometers

Properties: Detectable by human eye, rainbow colors

Uses: Vision, photography, photosynthesis

Ultraviolet (UV)

Wavelength: 10 to 380 nanometers

Properties: Ionizing radiation, invisible to human eye

Uses: Sterilization, tanning, detecting forged documents

X-Rays

Wavelength: 0.01 to 10 nanometers

Properties: High energy, penetrating

Uses: Medical imaging, security scanners, astronomy

Gamma Rays

Wavelength: Less than 0.01 nanometers

Properties: Highest energy, shortest wavelength

Uses: Medical imaging, cancer treatment, sterilization
